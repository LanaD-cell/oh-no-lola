Bubbles the Little Blowfish

Deep in the sparkling blue waters of the Coral Cove lived a little blowfish named Bubbles. Bubbles was round and golden, with tiny fins that wiggled like ribbons when she swam. But there was one thing about Bubbles that made her stand out more than her golden glow—she had a temper that could blow her right up! When Bubbles got upset, she puffed herself into a big, spiky ball. It all started one sunny morning when Bubbles was playing hide-and-seek with her friends. Clownfish Charlie hid in the kelp, Octavia the octopus disguised herself as a rock, and shy Stella the seahorse floated perfectly still behind a coral fan. Bubbles swam around, searching and searching. But no matter where she looked, she couldn’t find anyone. "This is impossible!" she huffed, her cheeks puffing up. Suddenly—poof! Bubbles’ tiny body expanded into a huge spiky ball. She floated there, bobbing in frustration, while her friends peeked out from their hiding spots, giggling. "Bubbles, you’re supposed to stay calm and keep looking," said Octavia, wrapping a friendly tentacle around her. "I can’t help it!" Bubbles cried. "Whenever I feel mad or frustrated, I just… blow up!" Her friends didn’t mind too much, but Bubbles felt embarrassed. She didn’t want to keep losing her temper.

The Wise Old Turtle

The next day, Bubbles swam to the edge of Coral Cove to visit Old Tilda, the wise sea turtle. Everyone said Tilda knew the secrets of the ocean. "Hello, Bubbles," said Tilda with a gentle smile. "Why the long face?" Bubbles explained her problem, telling Tilda how she always puffed up when she got upset. "Ah, I see," said Tilda, nodding slowly. "Your puffing is a special gift, Bubbles. It keeps you safe when you’re scared or in danger. But you don’t need to puff up for every little thing, do you?" Bubbles shook her head. Tilda swam to a patch of sea grass and plucked a single blade. "Try this," she said. "When you feel angry or frustrated, take a deep breath and blow into this sea grass. Imagine you're blowing all your anger out into the water." Bubbles thought it sounded silly, but she promised to try.

Practice Makes Perfect

The next day, Bubbles joined her friends for a game of bubble tag. She was having fun until Charlie accidentally bumped her into a clump of seaweed. "Hey!" Bubbles shouted. She felt her cheeks start to puff. But then she remembered Tilda’s advice. Quickly, she grabbed a blade of sea grass and took a deep breath. She blew all her frustration into the water. Tiny bubbles floated up, carrying her anger away. "Sorry, Bubbles!" Charlie said. "It’s okay," Bubbles replied, smiling. Her friends cheered. "You didn’t puff up!" said Stella. Bubbles felt proud. From then on, she practiced blowing bubbles whenever she felt upset. Soon, she didn’t need the sea grass at all—just a big, calming breath.

Bubbles’ Big Moment

One day, a hungry eel slithered into Coral Cove. The other fish panicked, scattering in all directions. But Bubbles stayed calm. She took a deep breath, puffed herself up, and floated right in front of the eel. The eel took one look at the big, spiky Bubbles and slithered away, deciding Coral Cove wasn’t worth the trouble. Bubbles’ friends cheered. "You saved us, Bubbles!" From that day on, Bubbles was the hero of Coral Cove. She had learned to control her temper, but she also knew how to use her puffing power when it really mattered. And so, Bubbles swam happily through Coral Cove, proud of her golden glow, her spiky puff, and her big, brave heart.
